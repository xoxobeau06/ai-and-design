Understanding Visual Systems

Clarity and flexibility, not limitations.

Tokens

The irreducible core values. Colors, type sizes, and spacing units that act as your design vocabulary.

Components

Interactive building blocks. Buttons, inputs, and cards built from tokens to ensure strict consistency.

Hierarchy

The established visual order that guides a user's eye and explains the relationship between elements.

Modular Design

Patterns designed to snap together predictably, ensuring that the system can scale seamlessly.

The Research Journey

A five-week exploration of design organization and authorship.

Week 1

Dissecting System Logic

Exploring how design systems reveal the logic behind visual communication. We strip away the stylistic surface to see the mathematical rhythms underneath.

Week 2

Reference as Curatorial Authorship

Studying how collecting and organizing references becomes a creative act. The references you choose dictate the boundaries of your system.

Week 3

Constraint as Framework

Learning how constraints guide design decisions and encourage creativity. When everything is possible, nothing gets done. Constraints provide friction.

Week 4

Documentation as Interpretation

Understanding how documenting your process fundamentally changes how you view your own work. Writing rules forces clarity.

Week 5

Synthesizing & Building

Combining all insights into a structured toolkit for creating visual systems, moving from academic theory to practical application.

The Step-by-Step Building Process

  1. 01

    Compare Existing Systems

    Deconstruct established frameworks to understand why they work. You cannot build a system if you cannot read one.

  2. 02

    Collect References

    Gather inspiration intentionally. Don't just save images—annotate exactly what typographic or structural choices you are capturing.

  3. 03

    Define Constraints

    Establish the rules early. Select a limited typography scale, exact spacing measurements, and core colors before building components.

  4. 04

    Document Decisions

    Write down why a rule exists. If you change a rule, explain what led to that change. A system fails without a record of intention.

  5. 05

    Build the Structure

    Begin assembling primitives into components and, finally, into complete page structures, referencing the rules established in step three.

AI as a System Collaborator

AI is a brilliant pattern-matcher but a terrible author. It can accelerate your workflow without replacing your creative viewpoint.

Use the AI Collaboration Protocol to maintain control:

  • Brainstorming: Generate naming conventions or token aliases rapidly.
  • Pattern Recognition: Surface structural overlaps you might have missed in your logic.
  • Information Organization: Group massive component sets logically.

The designer defines the constraints; the AI permutes. The designer curates the final result.
